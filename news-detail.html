ITF Announces Comprehensive Youth Development Program for Traditional Sports

New initiative aims to engage young athletes worldwide and preserve traditional sporting heritage for future generations

Young athletes participating in traditional Turon sports training at the ITF Youth Academy in Tashkent

The International Turon Federation (ITF) today announced the launch of its most ambitious youth development program to date, designed to introduce traditional Turon sports to a new generation of athletes while preserving the cultural heritage these sports represent.

The comprehensive program, titled "Heritage Champions: Building Tomorrow's Traditional Athletes," will be implemented across all 28 member countries over the next three years, with an initial investment of $2.5 million from the ITF Development Fund.

Program Objectives

The initiative focuses on three core objectives: increasing youth participation in traditional sports, developing coaching expertise at the grassroots level, and creating sustainable pathways for young athletes to progress from local clubs to international competition.

"We recognize that the future of traditional sports lies in the hands of our youth," said Dr. Akmal Saidov, ITF President. "This program represents our commitment to ensuring that these ancient sporting traditions continue to thrive in the modern world while providing meaningful opportunities for young people to connect with their cultural heritage."

"Traditional sports are not just about competition – they are about identity, community, and the preservation of our shared human heritage. Through this program, we are investing in both athletic excellence and cultural continuity." - Prof. Elena Kozlova, ITF Vice President

Key Program Components

The development program encompasses several key components designed to create a comprehensive support system for young athletes:

  • Youth Academy Network: Establishment of regional training centers in each member country, equipped with traditional sports facilities and staffed by certified coaches.
  • Scholarship Program: Merit-based scholarships for promising young athletes to attend intensive training camps and international competitions.
  • Coach Education Initiative: Comprehensive training and certification program for youth coaches, focusing on traditional techniques and modern sports science.
  • School Integration Program: Partnerships with educational institutions to introduce traditional sports into physical education curricula.

International Collaboration

The program emphasizes international collaboration, with planned exchange programs allowing young athletes to train in different countries and experience various cultural approaches to traditional sports. This cross-cultural element is designed to foster international friendship while maintaining the authentic character of each sporting tradition.

Member countries will work together to develop standardized training materials and coaching methodologies, ensuring consistency in instruction while respecting regional variations in traditional practices.

Implementation Timeline

The program will be rolled out in three phases over the next three years. Phase One, beginning in June 2024, will focus on establishing youth academies in founding member countries and developing core training materials. Phase Two will expand the program to all member countries and launch the international exchange component. The final phase will evaluate program effectiveness and plan for long-term sustainability.

Registration for the first youth academy programs will open in May 2024, with priority given to athletes aged 12-18 who demonstrate both athletic potential and commitment to traditional sports values.

Looking Forward

The ITF expects this program to significantly increase youth participation in traditional sports and create a pipeline of talented athletes for future international competitions. Beyond athletic development, the program aims to strengthen cultural connections and promote international understanding through sport.

"This is more than a sports development program," noted Mr. Jamshid Karimov, ITF Secretary General. "It's an investment in cultural preservation and international cooperation. We're not just training athletes; we're nurturing cultural ambassadors who will carry these traditions forward for generations to come."
